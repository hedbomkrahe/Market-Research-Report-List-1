<p><p>Vitamin B3, also known as niacin or nicotinic acid, is an essential water-soluble vitamin that plays a critical role in energy metabolism, DNA repair, and cellular signaling. It exists in two main forms: nicotinic acid and nicotinamide, both of which are precursors to coenzymes NAD and NADP, vital for numerous biochemical reactions in the body. Vitamin B3 is widely used in dietary supplements, pharmaceuticals, and food fortification to address deficiencies and support overall health.</p><p>The Vitamin B3 market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing awareness of its health benefits, rising demand for dietary supplements, and its application in treating conditions like pellagra and high cholesterol. Additionally, the pharmaceutical sector is exploring its potential in treating neurological disorders and metabolic syndromes. Geographically, North America and Europe dominate the market, while Asia-Pacific is emerging as a high-growth region due to increasing disposable incomes and healthcare awareness.</p></p>
