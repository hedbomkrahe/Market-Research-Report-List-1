<p><p>Vacuum Assisted Closure (VAC) therapy is a wound care technique that utilizes a vacuum dressing to promote healing in various types of wounds. By applying controlled negative pressure, VAC therapy enhances blood flow to the wound area, removes excess fluid, and promotes the formation of granulation tissue. This approach is commonly used in treating chronic wounds, surgical wounds, and traumatic injuries, making it a vital tool in modern wound management.</p><p>The Vacuum Assisted Closure Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.4% during the forecast period, driven by rising incidences of chronic wounds, diabetic ulcers, and postoperative complications. Increasing awareness of advanced wound care technologies and the growing geriatric population are contributing factors to this growth. Moreover, technological advancements in VAC devices, such as portability and ease of use, are enhancing their adoption in both hospital settings and home care. </p><p>Latest trends in the market include the integration of digital solutions for monitoring wound healing progress, as well as the development of single-use disposable VAC devices, which offer convenience and reduce the risk of infection.
