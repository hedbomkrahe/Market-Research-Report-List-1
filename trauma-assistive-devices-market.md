<p><p>The Trauma Assistive Devices market includes various types designed for orthopedic applications. Intramedullary nails are internal devices for stabilizing fractures, while intramedullary hip screws provide support for femoral neck fractures. Conventional hip screws are used for hip stabilization, whereas external fixations stabilize fractures externally by connecting with pins. Bone pins serve to align and secure broken bones, and "others" encompasses a range of additional devices catering to different trauma management needs. Each type plays a vital role in enhancing recovery and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
