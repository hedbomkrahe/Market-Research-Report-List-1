<p><p>The automotive auxiliary brake market includes two primary types: drum brakes and disc brakes. Drum brakes utilize a cylindrical drum and shoes that press against the drum's inner surface to create friction, offering efficient braking in various conditions. Disc brakes, on the other hand, feature a rotor and caliper arrangement, providing better heat dissipation and more consistent braking performance. Both types are crucial for enhancing vehicle safety and stability, especially in heavy-duty applications where reliable braking systems are essential.</p></p>
